I am a security engineer and architect with over 20 years of hands-on experience designing, engineering, and operating enterprise-scale security, infrastructure, and operational platforms. My work spans Enterprise Vulnerability Management, External Attack Surface Management, Web Application Scanning, Operational Technology (OT) and Industrial IoT security, Network Access Control (802.1X), Managed Security Services, Endpoint Detection and Response, and global security operations across large enterprises.

At Verizon, I mapped all Verizon networks and subnets across all Verizon Business Units, broken out by geographical location, manually normalizing over two million lines of unstructured raw data into network, country, state, site code, subnet, and CIDR block. This work created the first enterprise-wide view of subnet distribution, enabling Splunk visualizations such as counts of subnets across all 50 U.S. states and other telemetry that had never existed before, providing at-a-glance visibility of enterprise address space.

I expanded Verizon’s enterprise vulnerability scanning program across Verizon cloud platforms to allow network scanning in AWS (Production/Non Production), Microsoft Azure (Production/Non Production), Oracle Cloud (Production/Non Production), and Google Cloud (Production/Non Production) environments. I also executed enterprise-scale deployment of 410,000+ Tenable Nessus Agents, delivering comprehensive host-based vulnerability visibility across supported Microsoft Windows and Linux operating systems, including credentialed Solaris environments, expanding assessment depth beyond traditional network scanning.

I co-developed the Agent Proliferation Dashboard (APD) used to inform asset owners whether Tenable Nessus Agent, Crowdstrike Falcon, Symantec DCS, Flexera BDNA, OpenText Encase, HCL BigFix, and Splunk Universal Forwarder are installed correctly and actively reporting.

I was the primary architect and engineer of Armis Centrix™, creating operational capabilities that did not exist natively in the Armis platform, and built comprehensive reporting directly from Armis APIs to provide appliance health, collector-to-integration operational monitoring, usable reporting, and asset prioritization.

I also created the External Asset Visibility Management Reporting Splunk Application, providing a single consolidated view of external asset visibility by normalizing and correlating data from Tenable ASM, Cycognito, and BitSight—replacing fragmented tool-specific views with unified, consumable reporting.

Earlier in my career, I worked as a hands-on engineer building and operating foundational security and infrastructure technologies at scale. I contributed to the development and release of Network ICE BlackICE enterprise intrusion detection software, including work on high-performance network drivers and protocol analysis components capable of sustaining very high packet rates without loss across Windows and Linux platforms.

I collaborated on early wireless security research and tooling, including WEPCrack and the Wireless Assessment Toolkit, combining custom Linux-based tooling, GPS integration, and field-deployable assessment platforms to support real-world wireless penetration testing and security evaluation.

My experience also includes deep, practical engineering of enterprise network access and endpoint control technologies. I engineered and implemented Network Access Control (802.1X) environments using Cisco ACS, Aruba infrastructure, and Sygate endpoint technologies, securing large-scale enterprise networks and enforcing device authentication across wired and wireless environments.

In parallel, I built and supported enterprise systems and operational platforms, including MRP and MRP II application development, real-time business module engineering, job scheduling, release management using Rapid Application Development toolsets, and generation of historical operational and trend reporting for manufacturing and automotive environments including New Venture Gear, Eaton, and Chrysler Corporation.

Across every stage of my career—from low-level protocol and driver engineering, to enterprise security platforms, to large-scale vulnerability and asset visibility programs—the common thread has been hands-on engineering focused on accuracy, operational clarity, and turning complex technical data into systems that organizations can actually use.
